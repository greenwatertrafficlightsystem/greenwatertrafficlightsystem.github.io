Application Letter - Fleet Management Trainees - 2nd Assignment

Project TSI on MIA Project

Since planning to join Boskalis as a Fleet Management Trainee, the idea of being hands on working on a project, on a multi disciplanry role was one of the ideas that resonated on my mind. Indeed, as I have discussed with my internal coach Gaby Steentjes, on some positions, a generalist can be someone that provides a drone view to a project. In my opinion, I think this is one of the positions where a multiskilled colleague can become a key partner on the following six months for the project.

OPENNESS TO DEVELOP DIFFERENT TASKS
Since I decided I wanted to work for Boskalis, I understood that my Nautical background might not be the most handy on a company that does not perform pure shipping and fleet management, but complex engineering projects. Therefore, I understood that I could make from my handicap an opportunity to learn and grow on several subjects where I own a little of expertise, but not enought to call myself expert on electronics, mechanics or hydraulics systems.

HANDS ON PRACTICAL TRAINEESHIP

However, if I think of my self on my previous role as AB Seaman, I can see myself doing works on the three aforementioned specialization, helping the engineers on repairing the valves, pipes, engines, or doing whichever job that might be needed. Although by that time I was just assisting, I already felt that being analitical, understanding equipment and reaching manufacturers and sueprintendents when a hand is needed, was something I was open to do, and that´s what for me means being practical and focusing on a practical traineeship.

INTEREST ON OPERATIONAL ASPECTS
Indeed, and as I expressed previously to my colleagues, on this first assignment I missed a bit of experience on operational matters. I see this assignment, as a good opportunity to get this real field experience and grow from the Fleet Management perspective to a more operational profile, that could lead me to better understand Boskalis.

I can also think that I came to Boskalis due to my Nautical background, looking forward an opportunity to complete part of my compulsory deck cadet sea time to achieve my 2nd Office licence. I strongly believe that this assignment, as explained during the introduction meeting, can fit on doing part of a rotation onboard one of our dredgers as Deck Cadet. This would give me a deep understanding on how our dredgers operate, while performing repair and maintenance on the vessel and understanding project needs.

SUMMARY

I think this opportunity fits well on my portfolio of activities within Boskalis. Being abroad is a challengue I am eager to give a try a chance. Indeed, because as previously mentioned, I ahve seafaring background, I understand that is a rotation scheme that might fit my work - life balance. On the other side, the tasks proposed to do during the presentation, plus the twist and further step I do of this assignment onboard a dredger, makes of it a perfect fit for my next 6 months within Boskalis.
