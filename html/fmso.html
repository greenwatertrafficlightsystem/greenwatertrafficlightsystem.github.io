Fleet Management Trainees - 2nd Assignment

FLEET MANAGEMENT SYSTEM UPDATE

As part of the key tasks for the ISM Compliance team, maintaining and updating a SAfety Management System is essential to ensure compliance with the highest standars but also to ensure are vessels are allowed to sail and hired by our client. During the last quarter of the year, ISM Compliance team, usually ends a process of information gathering and management of change on each of the procedures that require revision.

IMPROVE SAFETY ON THE AFT DECK OF ANCHOR HANDLING TUGS
With the afore mentioned title, I was given the first assignment of the traineeship. Mainly, hardware measures were being investigated in order to understand wether they should be implemented or not. Some of them required a from a in depth study while others just required from reaching out suppliers if they could supply the equipment considered to improve safety. Indeed, one of the tools implemented was this Stiffy tool in order to manage lifting cargo. This tool allows our colleague to operate the cargo and to ensure that cargo is kept away from crashing, or being positioned in the wrong spot, while giving a safe margin away from a lifted cargo.

AND WHERE DOES IT FIT ON THE FLEET MANAGEMENT SYSTEM?
In the Fleet Management System we had a supporting document called Safety and Environmental Catalogue (706C if you want to dig deeper). Basically it is an Excel sheet, where arranged in tabs, we display several kinds of equipment with their Maximo numbers, so a vessel know which item should they put when they make a Purchase Order (P.O.). During the month of November, I worked on implementing this tools as part of the FMSO, apart from assisting some other colleagues who addressed other needs within our Offshore Fleet to be added onto the catalogue.

Indeed, this supporting document needed an update as no every supporting document is updated every year. Plenty of items were updated attending to vessel needs and it is available for the 2023 FMSO.
